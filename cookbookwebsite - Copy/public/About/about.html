About Me And My Love For Cooking










This book is a record of my favourite recipes, collected since I started cooking in my late teens and discovered the simple pleasures and adventures of cooking.

Believe it or not it started with the Woman’s Weekly Cooking Class Cookbook, (bought in 1976 for $3.98) as I wanted to cook Beef Wellington which was on the cover. I still have my original book which is very dog eared and stained and the book has come away from its cover, but I would never part with it. Over the years I have cooked at least 50% of the recipes in that cookbook.

My go to cookbook is Margaret Fulton’s Encyclopaedia of Food and Cookery 1983, it covers just about everything. It’s a hardcover so it has handled the wear and tear better with quite a few notations on the pages.

These recipes are from family, friends, many cookbooks, various cooking shows, magazines and some of my own original recipes. It includes my family’s favourite recipes as a record for them, some passed down from several generations. Many of these recipes were collected when my children were young, I was working part-time, and busy with family demands.

They are mostly simple and easy to follow, yet very tasty and fail safe even for the beginner cook.

Some recipes I have tweaked to my liking, remember that recipes are your guide but experimenting is fun and most importantly enjoy the adventure with food.

Happy cooking, Leanne
